NDA Eligibility Criteria:

    NDA 1 Eligibility:

    → Candidates who have passed Class 12 can apply for the Army wing of NDA.

    → Those who have passed Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics can apply for Air Force and Naval wings of NDA. Candidates who are appearing in the Class 12 can also apply.

    NDA 2 Eligibility:

    → Both male and female candidates can apply for the exam.

    → NDA 2 age limit is that candidates who are both not earlier than January 2, 2005, or later than January 1, 2008, are eligible to apply for the exam.

    → For the Army Wing of NDA, candidates must have passed Class 12 or equivalent from a recognised state education board or university.

    → For Air Force and Naval Wings of NDA, candidates must have passed Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

    → For the 10+2 Cadet Entry Scheme at the Indian Naval Academy, candidates must have passed Class 12 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

Documents Required for NDA Application form?

The documents that candidates need to upload are given below:

Scanned images of photograph and signature

• Photo-identity card in PDF format (Aadhar Card / Voter Card/PAN Card/ Passport/ Driving License/ School Photo ID/Any other photo ID Card issued by the State / Central Government)

• Marksheet and admit card of Class 10 and 12

Step-wise Process on How to Fill NDA Application Form

1) NDA Form : Part 1

Step 1- Candidate’s Registration

Candidates are required to enter their personal details, address, and educational qualifications. In the personal details part, candidates had to fill up their name, date of birth, father’s name, mother’s name, Aadhaar number, nationality, fee remission allowed, community, marital status, etc. There will be four options indicated by the drop-down menu to select education qualification. Candidates need to enter their complete address, email ID, and contact number.

Step 2: Choosing the branch preferences.

Applicants are to choose their branches: Indian Army, Air Force, and Navy

Mark your preference in the order of one to four

Candidates are also required to enter whether they are students of sanik/military school or son of JCO/NCO/other rank officer?

Click ‘Continue’ to move to the next page

Step 3 - Verify the details

Candidates are required to verify the details entered by them in the application form. No correction is allowed after the submission of the NDA form. After verifying the details, candidates are required to agree to the terms and conditions by clicking the required checkbox.

Step 4 - Generation of registration ID

The system displays the registration ID. The page also shows other details such as name, father’s name, DOB, email id, etc. With this, the Part I registration is completed. The registration ID is also sent to the candidate’s registered email ID.

2) NDA Form : Part 2

In part II, candidates need to pay the application fees, select the exam centre, upload scanned images of photographs and signatures. The steps to fill the Part II of NDA application form are given below:

NDA Online Form Fee:

• Applicants can pay their fee through cash/credit card/debit card or net banking.

• Those who opt for ‘Pay by Cash’ mode are required to take the printout of the system-generated Pay-in-slip during the Part-II registration. They are required to deposit the fee at the counter of SBI on the next working day.

• Candidates should note that the fees once paid are not refunded.

The structure of application fee is given below:

Category Fees (In INR)
General/OBC 100
SC,ST, Sons of JCOs, NCOs, ORs, Women Nil

Selection of exam centre:

• Candidates are required to choose NDA exam centre as per their suitability

• They can choose only three exam centres

• Allotment of centre will be on the first-apply-first-allot basis

Uploading of photograph, signature & photo identity proof:

• Candidates are required to upload the scanned images of photograph, signature, and photo-identity card

• The file should not exceed 300 kb and must be less than 20 kb

Before submitting the application form, candidates are required to agree to the declaration and submit the form. Candidates can view the application form by clicking View/Print button.

Frequently Asked Questions on NDA Application Form

Q- What is the age limit to apply for the NDA exam?

Applicants between the age group of 16.5 to 19.5 years can apply for the NDA exam.

Q-For what posts is the NDA exam held?

The NDA recruitment exam is conducted to recruit qualified candidates as officers in the Indian Defence Services.

Q- What is the academic qualification required for the NDA exam?

The basic educational qualification required to be eligible for the NDA exam is 10+2 pass.

Q- What are the stages of the NDA exam?

NDA Selection process has two stages, they are:

• Written exam

• SSB interview

Q-When is the NDA 1 application form 2024 to be released?

The NDA 1 registration form 2024 was released by UPSC on December 20, 2023.

Q- How can I apply for the NDA 2024?

Applicants can apply for the NDA 2024 exam by visiting the NDA official website - upsc.gov.in.

Q- When is the NDA 1 2024 exam scheduled to be held?

The NDA 1 exam 2024 will be conducted on April 21, 2024.

Q-When is the NDA 2 2024 exam scheduled to be held?

The NDA 2 2024 exam is scheduled to be held on September 1, 2024.
